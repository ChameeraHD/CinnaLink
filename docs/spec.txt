Sri Lanka is globally recognized as the leading producer and
exporter of true cinnamon, with industry operations heavily
dependent on skilled manual labor for activities such as
cutting,scraping, peeling, and quilling. Despite its importance,
workforce coordination in the cinnamon sector remains largely
informal,relying 
on 
word-of-mouth 
communication 
and
intermediaries. This results in limited worker reach, scheduling
conflicts, lack of transparency, and inefficient workforce
allocation.
To address these challenges, the CinnaLink platform is proposed
as 
a 
mobile- 
based 
digital 
workforce 
connection 
and
management system for the Sri Lankan cinnamon industry. The
platform enables landowners to publish job opportunities in
advance of harvesting periods and allows workers or worker
groups to discover, apply for, and participate in suitable jobs. The
system further supports automated availability validation to
prevent double booking,structured job selection when multiple
approvals occur, daily task progress reporting based on
cinnamon quill output, and performance evaluation through
ratings and analytics.

The development of CinnaLink follows the Object-Oriented
Software Development (OOSD) methodology. During the Object-
Oriented Analysis (OOA) phase, key domain entities within the
cinnamon workforce ecosystem are identified. These entities are
then structured and organized through Object-Oriented Design
(OOD) by defining class relationships, responsibilities, and system
modules. Finally, the system is implemented using Object-Oriented
Programming (OOP) principles such as encapsulation, abstraction,
inheritance, and polymorphism to ensure a modular, maintainable,
and scalable solution.

From the customer’s perspective, CinnaLink provides a
structured and scalable digital solution for workforce
coordination in the cinnamon industry. The system
reduces 
scheduling 
conflicts, 
improves 
operational
efficiency, enables performance-based worker evaluation,
and supports data-driven decision-making. Through
modular OOD architecture and secure implementation,
the platform ensures reliability, maintainability, and long-
term scalability, protecting the investment and enabling
future expansion.
From the user’s perspective, the system offers a
transparent and accessible platform for job discovery,
workforce hiring, and progress monitoring. Workers
benefit from fair job selection mechanisms, protection
from double booking, and recognition through ratings and
verification badges. Landowners gain structured job
management, real-time availability validation, and daily
production tracking to monitor workforce efficiency. The
user interface is designed to be simple and compatible
with commonly used mobile devices.

The Sri Lankan cinnamon industry faces several
workforce-related challenges:
Skilled workers are hired through informal networks
or middlemen, causing delays and higher costs.
Landowners struggle to find available workers
during peak harvesting periods.
Workers lack access to information about better-
paying or nearby job opportunities.
Time conflicts occur when workers accept multiple
jobs unknowingly (“double-booking”).
These issues lead to production delays, reduced
income for workers, and financial losses for
landowners.
